About

From research to the front line of business data

My path into analytics didn't start in a data team. I began as a journalist, then spent time as a university lecturer, before completing a PhD in Economics. That combination — asking sharp questions, teaching complex ideas clearly, and rigorous quantitative training — still shapes how I work today: dig into the real question first, then let the data (and the writing) do the explaining.

For the past 15+ years, I've worked as a data scientist and analytics leader across a wide range of business environments — contact centres, sales teams, operations, and government. That breadth means I've seen how the same underlying problems (messy data, manual workflows, decisions made on gut feel) show up differently depending on the business, and what actually works to fix them in each setting.

I work directly with business owners and leadership teams — there's no account manager between you and the person doing the analysis. My research has been published in over 10 peer-reviewed journals, and I bring that same rigor to commercial work: tactical solutions built on your own data, workflow knowledge, machine learning, and practical AI automation.

Recent work includes customer retention modelling, pricing optimisation, sales trend reporting, forecasting, contact-centre analytics, and AI-assisted workflow design. A small investment, applied to the right question or workflow, can create a disproportionately large outcome — especially when it removes repeated manual work.
